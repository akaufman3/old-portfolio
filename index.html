Google Chrome Browser Extension

Introducing Slimformation: a browser extension that promotes conscious consumption of information and helps avid readers visualize their information intake.

Duration: Completed from conceptualization within a 10 week quarter.

Role:

      - Largely involved in the conceptualization of the product including layout, color scheme, and chart information presentation (donut chart)
      - Conceptualized the browser extension logo design for the tool bar

      - Information presentation

  El Pulso Chicago: A new type of mobile app

Tagline: EL PULSO Chicago: Live to the beat of la ciudad

Duration of project: Product to be completed within the 10 weeks of the final graduate quarter of the Medill School of Journalism.

The Product:

    Website: http://www.elpulsochicago.com

You can view the product online. Once the site has finished loading on Firefox, select "Tools" from the browser navigation bar, then "Web Developer", then "Responsive Design View". The dimensions of the product are 320 X 480px.

Vision Statement:

EL PULSO Chicago moves to the beat of the Second City’s Hispanic-American community, delivering hyper-local mobile content on the arts, food and music that reflects our young audience’s cross-cultural identity. But EL PULSO Chicago is more than just an information hub -- it is also a space for our community to engage, explore and reinforce its vibrancy. At EL PULSO Chicago, we believe Hispanic and American no longer have to stand alone and that our audience can remain true to both cultures -- proving that, now more than ever, one size no longer fits all. But EL PULSO reaches beyond city limits. EL PULSO and its tailored model will easily expand to other metropolitan areas with young and thriving Hispanic-American communities -- so they can also live to the beat of their own ciudades.

Mission statement:

With an eye for the authentic, the creative and the dynamic, EL PULSO Chicago offers a mobile experience that the city’s young Hispanic-American community has never had before. EL PULSO is the only Chicago-based bilingual mobile app that young, engaged and technologically inclined Hispanic-Americans can turn to for art, food and music content reflective of their cross-cultural identities.

But at EL PULSO, we don’t just provide content -- we also use our mobile platform to empower our audience to share, engage and explore.

Elevator pitch:

EL PULSO Chicago is the only Chicago-based bilingual mobile-responsive website that acts like an app, delivering hyperlocal art, food and music content that reflects the cross-cultural identity of our young, engaged and technologically inclined Hispanic-American audience.

    Interactive Innovation Project

The Interactive Innovation Project lets students explore the latest digital tools and technology as they find new ways to improve the creation, distribution and consumption of journalism. Students collaborate to create a new publication for digital platforms and/or experiment with new technologies to learn how to apply them to journalism’s mission and challenges. Students in this class conduct original market research and gather information on best practices and innovations in other markets. They use this information – and their own creative insights – to develop ideas for new products that will be embraced by audiences and also offer a realistic chance of profitability.

Role:

      - Conceptualization of product
      - Mission and Vision statements
      - Audience research
      - Concept validation
      - Wireframing
      - A/B testing
      - Competitive Analysis
      - Positioning Chart
      - Business model preparations
      - Card Sorting
      - Architecture diagrams
      - Story Brainstorming
      - Design of homepage button interface
      - Mobile responsive design and coding
      - Marketing tactics

Projects Using APIs

Comparing cultures: Tokyo/NYC Flickr Search

Site: http://amelia-kaufman.com/jscriptlabs/searchTokyoNYC/searchTokyoNYC.html

This product was the result of a class assignment to “dream up a website that provides a dynamic experience based on the data it finds out on the web.” We were asked to build a page that programmatically requests data that you would need to deliver the experience. The point of the assignment was to demonstrate that we had learned how to read about API services and can figure out how to talk to them. This product was meant to examine cultural differences and to see how people around the world view everyday things from different perspectives. The user can see social parameters, varying customs, traditions and can use the product to spark discussions.

      Medill: Multi-platform Magazine Reframing Exercises

      *The following assignments were assigned by Medill Graduate Magazine Editing Associate Professor Patti Wolter, former senior features editor at Self Magazine.

    • Packaging/high-concept thinking in print and online:

      We were asked to complete the assignment over the course of a week. The assignment was to choose a magazine, create a feature to propose, articulate the main throughline for the story, identify key angles, identify key sources, and to identify how the story will be delivered – including a strong digital approach.

      Click the link below to download the proposal:

      Architectural Digest Story Proposal

    • Understanding your audience: Magazine Architecture and Advertising:

      For this assignment we had one week to complete a magazine map of one of our favorite magazines. We were asked to identify the pillars, features, common FOB, BOB, TOC, etc. of the magazine. As well, we were asked to analyze the digital strategy of the publication.

      Click the link below to download the magazine map:

      Wired Magazine Map

    • Story Reframing:

      This assignment was to be completed over the course of a week reframing a story from the magazine mapping assignment above.

      Click the link below to download the story reframing:

      Story Reframing Exercise

    • Magazine Recitation:

      We were asked in teams to prepare a recitation of a specific magazine summarizing the basic economic data (circulation, staffing, ad page rates, CPM, publishing history) as well as a description of the median reader (demographic and psychographic point of view), biography of chief editor, basic structure and design of the magazine, an analysis of the magazine’s main competitors and an analysis of the magazines digital strategy.

      Click the link below to download the magazine recitation:

      National Geographic Recitation

      For this assignment we worked in small groups to analyze the current state of video in the magazine industry and the role it is playing in transforming the way content is distributed and presented to readers.
